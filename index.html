01About

I came to Finland almost by accident. A search for the best universities in additive manufacturing led me to Aalto. I came for a degree, but my internship at Vaisala showed me a way of working I wanted to be part of: calm but serious, with real respect for people and their time. That is a big reason I am here for the long term, learning Finnish, working toward permanent residency, and trying to become part of the society rather than just live in it.

By nature I like to go deep and understand things at a fundamental level before building on them, so I am comfortable working independently. But I value teams just as much. Several minds together form a bigger, shared mind that achieves far more than any one person alone.

Before my studies I spent years training engineering teams and running demonstrations for industrial clients, including non-specialists. It taught me that understanding a problem only matters if you can explain it simply to the people who depend on it. I would rather get something working and useful than chase a perfect version forever.

PROJECT 01Industrial R&D
3D Scanning & Digital Metrology Feasibility Study — Vaisala R&D

During my summer project at Vaisala, I assessed how 3D scanning could be used for reverse engineering, inspection, jig design, and failure analysis. I scanned real parts, built case studies, compared software options, and recommended a practical workflow for future use.

  • Reverse engineering: scanned production tooling and rebuilt accurate parametric CAD for spare parts and supplier-independent redesign
  • Digital inspection: aligned scans to CAD to surface problem areas instantly, with quantifiable deviation data
  • Failure analysis: developed a before/after drop-test method, using colour maps to quantify deformation against a ±1 mm tolerance
  • Software benchmark: compared QuickSurface and Geomagic Design X, and recommended QuickSurface for its faster, SolidWorks-integrated workflow

PROJECT 02
Solar Panel Deployment Mechanism for a 6U CubeSat

This was my M.Sc. thesis project at Aalto University, for the Foresail-2 mission. I designed a passive mechanism to deploy two solar panels for a 6U CubeSat. The work covered concept selection, CAD design, prototype manufacturing, and deployment testing — built with workshop methods and off-the-shelf materials to prove the concept cheaply.

  • Defined the requirements from mission parameters and CubeSat standards
  • Selected a dual tape-spring hinge using a weighted decision matrix
  • Engineered the hinge geometry and built the full prototype
  • Tested deployment and repeated stow-release cycles

PROJECT 03
Educational Cutaway Display Models — Atomic Energy Commission

For this project, I helped create two large educational cutaway models from technical drawings: a vertical pump and a pressure vessel. I worked on CAD modelling, splitting parts for manufacturing, 3D printing, laser cutting, finishing, colour coding, and final assembly. Both models are now on display to teach visitors how the machines work inside.

  • Reconstructed the full internal geometry of both machines in CAD from client drawings
  • Split the oversized bodies into printer-sized segments with snap-clips and bonded joints, then built them across FDM printing, laser cutting and CNC machining
  • Hand-finished, colour-coded and assembled both floor-standing models on custom stands for museum display

PROJECT 04
CompositionUltra — Graphene-Membrane Gas Analyser

This was a product development project at Aalto Design Factory for a graphene startup. Our team developed a concept for a gas analyser using a graphene membrane. I worked on the mechanical layout, CAD design, prototype parts, and the final physical demonstrator.

  • Helped develop the core concept, linking gas permeation through porous graphene to an optically-read measurement
  • Designed the aluminium device body and laid out the internals around the membrane port, valves and electronics in a 150 × 100 × 50 mm footprint
  • Led the 3D printing, producing sectioned models and a finished demonstrator for the final exhibition

PROJECT 05
Prosthetic Socket Fabrication — Scan to Print

This was my bachelor's thesis project at UET Peshawar. I used 3D scanning and 3D printing to make custom prosthetic sockets with a faster, cheaper digital workflow that cut fabrication cost by ~88% and lead time by ~90%. The sockets were designed from patient limb scans, printed, fitted, and improved through testing.

  • 3D scanned patient limb geometry as the basis for a custom socket
  • Reconstructed CAD models of the socket and components from scan data
  • Prepared and optimised models for FDM additive manufacturing
  • Fabricated & fitted printed sockets directly on patients, iterating on design and print parameters
